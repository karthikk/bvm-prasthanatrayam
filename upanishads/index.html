The twelve principal Upanishads, representing the culmination of Vedic wisdom and the foundation of Vedantic philosophy

About the Principal Upanishads

The Upanishads, literally meaning "sitting down near" (a teacher), represent the philosophical culmination of the Vedic tradition. These twelve principal Upanishads, commented upon by Adi Shankaracharya, reveal the fundamental truth of non-dual Brahman and provide the direct path to Self-realization through knowledge, contemplation, and spiritual practice.
